# Eleanora Sefton

**Column:** *The Inheritance*

Mrs. Sefton writes on the intellectual scaffolding beneath contemporary debates — the philosophical, theological, and institutional inheritance that shapes how societies govern themselves and allocate resources, often without knowing it.

A reader of Tocqueville, Burke, and the longer tradition of Western political thought, she is especially interested in the questions that modern secular discourse has inherited from Christianity without acknowledgment, and in the recurring tension between technocratic administration and democratic self-governance. She believes that the most important developments in political economy are usually visible first in the arguments people have about seemingly unrelated subjects — and that the surest sign of a dying consensus is the vehemence with which its defenders insist it requires no defence.

Her occasional essays appear under the heading *The Inheritance*. She does not keep regular hours.
