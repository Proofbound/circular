# J. Alderton Vane

**Column:** *The Long View*

A former adviser to sovereign wealth funds and family offices across the Gulf and Southeast Asia, Mr. Vane writes on the structural forces — demographic, monetary, and political — that shape capital flows over decades rather than quarters.

His particular interests are the great-power competition between the United States and China, the political economy of energy transitions, and the recurring tendency of markets to price in the future they want rather than the one they are getting. He is skeptical of consensus forecasts and believes that the most consequential investment risks are precisely the ones that sound implausible when first described.

Mr. Vane's column, *The Long View*, appears in each issue of the Circular. He has been known to observe that the half-life of a "structural shift" is approximately eighteen months, after which it is reclassified as a "temporary adjustment."
