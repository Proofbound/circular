# H.C. Whitmore

**Column:** *The Machinery of Consequence*

Mr. Whitmore spent two decades building software products in East Asia before concluding that the interesting question about technology is never "what can it do?" but rather "what does it make people *want* to do?" He writes on the second-order effects of technical change — how new tools reshape labour markets, institutional structures, and the distribution of analytical authority.

A student of the history of automation from Jacquard looms to large language models, he is particularly interested in the moments when a technology stops augmenting human work and begins making the entire workflow irrelevant. He maintains that most commentary on artificial intelligence confuses the tool with the task, and that the people best positioned to understand what machines will do next are those who understand what people were *actually* doing before.

His column, *The Machinery of Consequence*, examines technology through the lens of economics and institutional behaviour rather than product reviews.
